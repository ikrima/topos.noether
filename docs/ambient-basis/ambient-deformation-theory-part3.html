§ 1

Analytic Setup

Let \(D \subset \mathbb{R}^d\) be a fixed bounded container domain, and let \(\Omega \subset D\) be a reference ambient world. Let \(\Theta \subset W^{1,\infty}(D;\mathbb{R}^d)\) be a small neighborhood of \(0\) such that for every \(\theta \in \Theta\), the perturbation map

\[T_\theta := I + \theta\]

is bi-Lipschitz. The deformed ambient world is \(\Omega_\theta := T_\theta(\Omega)\).

The Outside-In Formulation

Fix Banach spaces \(X, Y\). Let \(F : \Theta \times X \to Y\) be a \(C^1\) map encoding the transported realization law. The equation \(F(\theta, u) = 0\) means: "in the ambient world \(\Omega_\theta\), the realized object/state is \(u\)."

Here \(\theta\) is the world variable, \(u\) is the realized response variable.

§ 2

The Ambient Implicit Function Theorem

Theorem 2.1 — Ambient IFT

Assume \(F \in C^1(\Theta \times X, Y)\), there exists \(u_0 \in X\) with \(F(0, u_0) = 0\), and \(D_u F(0, u_0) : X \to Y\) is a Banach-space isomorphism.

Then there exist neighborhoods \(U \subset \Theta\), \(V \subset X\), and a unique \(C^1\) map \(u : U \to V\) such that

\[F(\theta, u(\theta)) = 0 \qquad \text{for all } \theta \in U\]

with first variation

\[Du(0)[\eta] = -\bigl(D_u F(0,u_0)\bigr)^{-1} D_\theta F(0,u_0)[\eta]\]

The operator \(\mathcal{R} := -(D_u F)^{-1} D_\theta F\) is the ambient response operator. It maps ambient perturbations to realized object variations — the rigorous heart of ambient deformation theory.

§ 3

Hadamard Reduction

Theorem 3.1 — Hadamard Structure Theorem

Let \(J\) be a shape-differentiable functional. If \(\theta_1, \theta_2 \in W^{1,\infty}\) have the same normal trace on \(\partial\Omega\), then

\[DJ(\Omega)(\theta_1) = DJ(\Omega)(\theta_2)\]

Tangential boundary deformations are first-order invisible. The shape gradient concentrates on the boundary-normal component:

\[DJ(\Omega)(\theta) = \int_{\partial\Omega} g_J \, (\theta \cdot n) \, d\sigma\]

The effective infinitesimal ambient deformation space is the quotient \(\mathcal{A}_\Omega^{(1)} = W^{1,\infty}(D;\mathbb{R}^d) / \mathcal{T}_\Omega\), where \(\mathcal{T}_\Omega\) is the tangential gauge subspace. This is the first rigorous ambient basis: boundary-normal deformation modes modulo tangential gauge.

§ 4

Observables and Induced Deformation

Proposition 4.1

For a \(C^1\) observable \(G : \Theta \times X \to Z\), the realized observable \(\mathcal{J}(\theta) = G(\theta, u(\theta))\) satisfies

\[D\mathcal{J}(0)[\eta] = D_\theta G[\eta] + D_u G \cdot Du(0)[\eta] = D_\theta G[\eta] - D_u G \cdot (D_u F)^{-1} D_\theta F[\eta]\]

This is the general formula: the ambient world deforms, and observables inherit that deformation through the solution operator. Direct ambient effect plus indirect effect through the response operator.

§ 5

Categorical Lift

Definition 5.1 — Category AmbRel

Objects are tuples \(\bigl(B_{\mathrm{in}} \xrightarrow{i_{\mathrm{in}}} E \xleftarrow{i_{\mathrm{out}}} B_{\mathrm{out}},\; M \hookrightarrow E,\; \sigma\bigr)\) where \(E\) is the ambient world, \(B_{\mathrm{in}}, B_{\mathrm{out}}\) are interface ports, \(M \hookrightarrow E\) is an embedded object, and \(\sigma\) is ambient decoration.

Morphisms are commuting diagrams carrying ports to ports, worlds to worlds, and objects to objects.

Structured cospans \(L(B_{\mathrm{in}}) \to E \leftarrow L(B_{\mathrm{out}})\) form a symmetric monoidal double category. Ambient worlds with interfaces compose by pushout — gluing outputs to inputs.

§ 7 — Toy Model A

Channel-Width Deformation

The clean "ambient scalar parameter" example. Let \(\Omega_h = (0, L) \times (-h, h)\) and solve steady Stokes flow under pressure drop \(\Delta P\). Fully developed Poiseuille flow gives:

\[U_h(y) = \frac{\Delta P}{2\nu L}(h^2 - y^2), \qquad Q(h) = \frac{2\Delta P}{3\nu L} h^3\]
Proposition 7.1

\(\displaystyle\frac{dQ}{dh} = \frac{2\Delta P}{\nu L} h^2 > 0.\)

The ambient variable \(h\) controls the realized observable \(Q\) cubically.

∎

§ 8 — Toy Model B

Phase Field with Topology Change

The cleanest topological event. Define \(\Omega_a\) as two disks with a bridge of half-width \(a\):

\[\Omega_a = B_1((-1.5,0)) \cup B_1((1.5,0)) \cup \bigl([-1.5,1.5]\times[-a_+,a_+]\bigr), \quad a_+ = \max(a,0)\]

Then \(\beta_0(\Omega_a) = 2\) if \(a < 0\), and \(\beta_0(\Omega_a) = 1\) if \(a > 0\). Connectivity switches.

Proposition 8.2 — Topology-Induced Coupling Switch

Let \(f \geq 0\) be supported in the left lobe. Then:

1. If \(a < 0\): the solution \(p_a\) is identically zero on the right lobe.

2. If \(a > 0\): the solution is nontrivial on the right lobe — signal propagates through the bridge.

∎
