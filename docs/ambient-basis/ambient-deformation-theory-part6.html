PART VI — TOY MODEL E

Ricci Flow Meets Euler

Geometry evolves by curvature. Fluid follows.

The ambient world is a flat torus \(\mathbb{T}^2\) equipped with an evolving conformal metric \(g_t = e^{2f(y,t)}(dx^2 + dy^2)\). Ricci flow smooths \(f\). An incompressible Euler fluid lives on this moving geometry. The realized velocity is whatever the current metric allows the momentum to become.

§ 2

Ricci Flow on the Conformal Factor

In 2D, Ricci flow \(\partial_t g = -2\,\mathrm{Ric}(g)\) stays in the conformal class. For \(f = f(y,t)\) only, it reduces to:

\[\partial_t f = e^{-2f}\,f_{yy}\]

This is a nonlinear diffusion equation. The geometry itself is being diffused — Ricci flow smooths curvature irregularities.

Gaussian Curvature
\[K = -e^{-2f}\,f_{yy}\]

Positive \(f_{yy}\) gives negative curvature (defocusing). Ricci flow drives \(K \to 0\) — the torus homogenizes.

§ 3

Forced Euler Shear on the Torus

Place an incompressible Euler fluid \(u = U(y,t)\,\partial_x\) on \((M, g_t)\), driven by a harmonic 1-form forcing \(G(t)\,dx\). The forced Euler equation collapses to:

\[\partial_t\bigl(e^{2f}\,U\bigr) = G(t)\]

Define the momentum density \(m(y,t) := e^{2f(y,t)}\,U(y,t)\). Then \(m_t = G(t)\), and:

\[U(y,t) = e^{-2f(y,t)}\left(m_0(y) + \int_0^t G(s)\,ds\right)\]

The fluid reacts to evolving geometry by pointwise rescaling through \(e^{-2f}\). Regions where the conformal factor is large suppress velocity; where it is small, velocity is amplified.

§ 5

The Geometric Gain Field

The map from momentum to velocity is \(m \mapsto U = e^{-2f}\,m\). The conformal factor acts as a moving gain field:

\[\text{gain}(y,t) = e^{-2f(y,t)}\]

Even with spatially uniform forcing, the realized velocity stays spatially nonuniform until Ricci flow homogenizes \(f\). The gain field is the geometric lens through which forcing becomes motion.

§ 6

Circulation vs. Velocity

Covector Circulation
\[\Gamma(y,t) = \oint_{S^1_x} \alpha = L\,e^{2f}\,U = L\,m(y,t)\]

Hence \(\partial_t \Gamma = L\,G(t)\). Covector circulation grows uniformly.

But vector velocity \(U = e^{-2f}\,m\) is redistributed by the evolving metric.

Forcing controls circulation. Ricci flow controls how that circulation is realized as actual velocity. This is the object/world split in its purest form.

The Cleanest Takeaway

The primitive state is not \(u\), but \((M, g_t, \alpha_t, u_t)\). The core formula:

\[U(y,t) = e^{-2f(y,t)}\left(m_0(y) + \int_0^t G(s)\,ds\right), \qquad f_t = e^{-2f}\,f_{yy}\]

The world evolves by curvature. The fluid momentum is injected simply. The realized velocity is whatever the current geometry allows that momentum to become. Mold first, filling second.
