§ 3–4

The Metric-Dependent Stokes Equation

Seek steady flow \(u = U(y)\,\partial_x\) driven by constant pressure gradient \(G > 0\).

Proposition 1 — Curved Stokes ODE
\[\bigl(a(y)^3\,U'(y)\bigr)' = -\frac{G}{\mu}\,a(y), \qquad U(\pm 1) = 0\]

The viscous operator depends on the metric through the weight \(a^3\). Flux:

\[Q[a] = \int_{-1}^{1} a(y)\,U(y)\,dy\]

The velocity profile \(U(y)\) and the flux \(Q\) respond to the metric \(a(y)\) — not to wall motion. The viscous operator itself is geometric.

§ 6

Flat World Recovery

When \(a(y) \equiv 1\): \(K = 0\), the ODE collapses to \(U'' = -G/\mu\), giving classical Poiseuille:

\[U_0(y) = \frac{G}{2\mu}(1 - y^2), \qquad Q[1] = \frac{2G}{3\mu}\]

The model is a genuine geometric generalization of plane Poiseuille flow.

§ 7

Small-Curvature Perturbation

Let \(a_\varepsilon(y) = 1 + \varepsilon\,\phi(y)\). Then \(K_\varepsilon = -\varepsilon\,\phi'' + O(\varepsilon^2)\). The first-order flux correction is:

Proposition 2 — First-Order Sensitivity
\[Q_1 = \frac{G}{\mu}\int_{-1}^{1}(1 - 4y^2)\,\phi(y)\,dy\]

In curvature form, with \(\phi(\pm 1) = 0\):

\[Q_1 = \frac{G}{\mu}\int_{-1}^{1}\Psi(y)\,K_1(y)\,dy, \qquad \Psi(y) = \frac{2y^4 - 3y^2 + 1}{6}\]

§ 8

The Sensitivity Kernel Ψ(y)

The weight \(\Psi(y) = (2y^4 - 3y^2 + 1)/6\) is the geometric lens for viscous transport.

It is positive near the center and vanishes at the walls. So positive curvature concentrated at the center increases flux, while curvature near the walls has diminished or reversed effect.

sensitivity kernel Ψ(y) — the geometric lens

The ambient world acts as a geometric lens for viscous transport. Where curvature is placed determines how throughput changes. This is the object/world coupling in its fluid-geometric form.

§ 9

Euler: Curvature as Pressure-Balancing

For inviscid steady flow \(u = U(y)\,\partial_x\) on the same metric, the Christoffel connection produces a transverse pressure gradient:

\[\partial_y p = a(y)\,a'(y)\,U(y)^2\]

Even with purely axial velocity, curvature induces transverse pressure. Curvature is not passive scenery — it enters the momentum balance directly.
