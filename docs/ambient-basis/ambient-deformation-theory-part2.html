§ 7

Knot Complements in Three Dimensions

The most vivid instance of negative-space topology is the knot complement. Given a smooth embedding \(K : S^1 \hookrightarrow S^3\), the complement \(S^3 \setminus K\) carries topological information that completely determines the knot — this is the Gordon–Luecke theorem.

The Knot Complement Principle

For a knot \(K \subset S^3\), the fundamental group \(\pi_1(S^3 \setminus K)\) — the knot group — is a complete invariant of prime knots. The object is fully determined by the topology of its negative space.

This is the ambient deformation thesis in its purest topological form: the complement encodes everything. Below, drag to rotate the trefoil knot and its tubular neighborhood. The translucent exterior is \(S^3 \setminus N_\varepsilon(K)\) — the space where the invariants live.

§ 7.1

Alexander Duality

Alexander duality provides the formal mechanism linking an embedded object to its complement. For a compact subspace \(K \subset S^n\):

\[\widetilde{H}_q(S^n \setminus K) \cong \widetilde{H}^{n-q-1}(K)\]

Homology of the complement is isomorphic to cohomology of the object (with a dimension shift). This is the topological incarnation of the Cast/Fill adjunction: information flows freely between the object and its negative space.

§ 8

The Moduli Landscape

The correct moduli space is \(\mathrm{Def}^{\mathrm{amb}}(M \hookrightarrow E, \sigma)\) — not merely the space of embeddings, but the space of ambient realizations, where two points are equivalent only after comparing both embedding data and negative-space profiles.

Definition 6 — Ambient Moduli Space

The ambient moduli space is the quotient

\[\mathcal{M}^{\mathrm{amb}} = \{(i, \sigma) \mid F(i,\sigma) = 0\} \,/\, \mathrm{Gauge}\]

where the gauge group acts simultaneously on embeddings and ambient structures.

Visualize this as a landscape. Each point represents a different ambient realization of the same abstract object. Ridges separate topologically distinct regions. Valleys are stable configurations. The height encodes a natural energy functional.

§ 9

Structured Cospans — Composing Open Worlds

An open ambient realization is a structured cospan:

\[L(B_{\mathrm{in}}) \longrightarrow E \longleftarrow L(B_{\mathrm{out}})\]

The apex \(E\) is the world. The feet \(B_{\mathrm{in}}, B_{\mathrm{out}}\) are its ports — interfaces through which the system connects to other systems. Composition glues outputs to inputs via pushout. This is the natural language for channels, pipes, membranes, and interaction zones.

§ 10

Arnold's Geodesics on SDiff

Arnold's foundational insight: ideal incompressible fluid motion is geodesic motion on the infinite-dimensional group \(\mathrm{SDiff}(\Omega)\) of volume-preserving diffeomorphisms, equipped with the \(L^2\) metric (kinetic energy). Euler's equation is the geodesic equation on this Lie group.

Arnold's Theorem (1966)

The Euler equation for an ideal incompressible fluid on a Riemannian manifold \((M, g)\) is the geodesic equation on \(\mathrm{SDiff}(M)\) with the right-invariant \(L^2\) metric.

\[\partial_t \omega + \mathcal{L}_u \omega = 0, \qquad u = K[\omega], \qquad \nabla \cdot u = 0\]

The ambient deformation lift: instead of fixing the domain, let the Riemannian metric \(g_t\) on \(M\) co-evolve with the flow. The geodesic equation then couples to ambient geometry — vortices reshape their world.

§ 11

Ricci-Coupled Surface Evolution

The most speculative direction from Part I: evolve the ambient metric itself as a state variable. The schematic evolution law is:

\[\partial_t g = -2\alpha\,\mathrm{Ric}(g) + \beta\,\mathcal{T}(u) - \gamma\,Q(\phi)\]

The Ricci term smooths the world (as in Perelman's resolution of the Poincaré conjecture). The stress term \(\mathcal{T}(u)\) lets the fluid carve geometry. The obstacle term \(Q(\phi)\) pins certain regions. Below: a surface evolves under this coupled law.

§ 12

The Full Pipeline

The central conjecture of ambient deformation theory — phase-switch factorization — asserts that many realization problems factor through:

\[\underbrace{M}_{\text{abstract object}} \xrightarrow{\;\mathsf{Embed}\;} \underbrace{(M \hookrightarrow E, \sigma)}_{\text{ambient realization}} \xrightarrow{\;\mathsf{Cast}\;} \underbrace{C_\varepsilon(X)}_{\text{mold / negative space}} \xrightarrow{\;\mathsf{Fill}\;} \underbrace{M'}_{\text{realized object}}\]

If this holds, the computational burden sits in the middle term — the mold. Negative space is the main state, not a byproduct.

The Formal Core — Restated

The primitive object is \(X = (M, E, i, \sigma)\), not \(M\) alone. This produces a double tangent space, a negative-space profile, a 2-category of ambient realizations, an adjoint-like Cast/Fill duality, a fluid model with evolving channels, and — at the deepest level — a Ricci-coupled metric evolution where the world itself is a dynamical variable driven by the objects it contains.
