An interactive series

The Measure
of Light

You already do measure theory. You just call it importance sampling.

Every time a renderer reuses a neighbour's light path, it is transporting a measure from one pixel's integration domain into another's. The machinery of ReSTIR and GRIS — target functions, shift maps, Jacobians, MIS weights — is already, exactly, the machinery of measures and their pushforwards. This series takes that correspondence seriously and follows it to a working instrument.

The way in is a symptom every practitioner knows: the firefly — a bright, structured burst that reuse produces in a few stubborn places and almost nowhere else. We treat that "almost nowhere else" as the clue. The places fireflies refuse to leave turn out to be precisely the places where the contribution stops being a well-behaved density and becomes something singular, or where the domain itself tears along a visibility edge. Name those places, and the firefly stops being noise and becomes a reading off an instrument.

A reused sample is a measure in transit. The firefly is what you see when it was carried through the wrong lens.

From there the path is short and mechanical. If a sample is a measure, the shift that moves it is a lens; the gap between what the lens delivered and what the destination actually wanted is a single scalar, the residual; and once you can read that scalar you can act on it — reusing boldly where the measure says it is safe, refusing where it isn't — without ever biasing the estimate. Five parts, one continuous argument.

How this was made

One scene, one engine, verified before drawn

Every part runs the same small, real ReSTIR estimator — a 2D direct-lighting world with a glossy floor, an area light, a finite occluder, and a constant-speed camera pan — implemented as an actual unbiased resampling engine, not a scripted animation. Before any figure was drawn, every quantitative claim was checked numerically against a reference: the estimator's bias, the firefly statistics, the residual's correlation, the intervention's effect. Several claims were corrected by that process before you ever saw them, and the corrections are noted in the parts where they happened.

The colour palette is fixed and semantic across all five parts, so a hue always means the same kind of measure:

diffuse · absolutely continuous glossy near-singular delta / residual volume visibility boundary

What is claimed, and what is not

Each part ends by separating exactly what is mathematically exact from what is illustrative. The six-class decomposition and the residual are presented as a computational design for reasoning about transport, not as a theorem about the rendering equation; whether the synthesis is novel is left as a question for a literature search, not asserted. The intervention's gains in Part V are measured against naive reuse, because the exact-shift engine is already well-behaved on its own — the decomposition's real territory is the approximate-shift, multi-bounce, specular-transport world this flat slice only gestures at. The series is a floor plan, drawn carefully, not a finished building.
