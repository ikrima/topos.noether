Proving
It Correct

Movement I built weights that are bounded and sum to one. But a tidy weight is not a correct estimator. Here the supplement proves four things: the estimator is unbiased, the survivor's distribution converges to the target, its variance converges to that of perfect importance sampling, and even at finite M the error is provably penned in.

§ 1 · The standard

What "correct" must mean

An estimator earns trust on two counts. Unbiased: on average it returns the true integral, at every sample count, with no systematic error. Consistent: as we feed it more candidates, its answer concentrates — the distribution it samples from sharpens toward the target, and its variance falls toward the best any importance sampler could achieve.

The obstacle is the object Movement I leaned on without justifying: the unbiased contribution weight \(W\). It replaced \(1/p\), yet it is not a reciprocal density — it is a random variable, and the density \(p_Y\) of the resampled survivor is, in general, impossible to write down. How can an estimator built on an uncomputable density possibly be correct? §S.5 answers, theorem by theorem. We take them in the order they earn their keep.

§ 2 · Theorem A.1

The weight that isn't a density

A random variable \(W\) is an unbiased contribution weight for \(X\) when, for every integrable \(f\),

\[ \mathbb{E}\bigl[f(X)\,W\bigr]=\int_{\operatorname{supp}X} f(x)\,\mathrm dx. \]

Theorem A.1 proves this is equivalent to the pointwise condition \(\mathbb{E}[W\mid X=x]=1/p_X(x)\). The proof (§S.5.1) runs through an indicator \(\mathbf 1_A\) and the law of total expectation: integrating the condition over any measurable set forces \(p_X(x)=p_X(x)^2\,\mathbb{E}[W\mid X{=}x]\), and dividing by \(p_X\) gives the reciprocal. So \(W\) need not be \(1/p\); it need only average to it, conditionally. That single relaxation is what lets resampling chain across passes whose densities no one can evaluate.

The demonstration below runs honest RIS: draw \(M\) candidates, resample one survivor \(Y\), hand it the weight \(W_Y = \tfrac{1}{\hat p(Y)}\sum_i w_i\). That \(W_Y\) is visibly a random variable — its histogram is broad — yet the running average of \(f(Y)\,W_Y\) converges to the exact integral. Change the test integrand: it converges every time, because \(W_Y\) is a contribution weight for \(Y\), not for one chosen \(f\).

§ 3 · §S.7.1

Where the integral lives

Unbiasedness is a claim about a domain: the estimator integrates \(f\) over \(\operatorname{supp}Y\), the set of outputs the resampler can actually produce. §S.7.1 pins that set exactly.

\[ \operatorname{supp}Y \;=\; \operatorname{supp}\hat p \;\cap\; \bigcup_{i=1}^{M} T_i\!\bigl(\operatorname{supp}X_i\bigr)\qquad\text{(Eq. 15 / S.108)} \]

A point survives only if the target gives it positive density and some shifted candidate can reach it. The proof splits into an easy case — a weight is positive exactly when its candidate is in-domain and its shifted image is in \(\operatorname{supp}\hat p\) — and a general case, which additionally permits \(w_i=0\) where the contribution \(c_i\) or the weight \(W_i\) vanishes, without disturbing the support identity. Drag the regions; the integral is collected only over their overlap.

§ 4 · Theorem A.2

Where the survivor lands

As candidates accumulate, the survivor \(Y\) stops being a rough draft and becomes a faithful draw from the normalized target \(\bar p = \hat p/\lVert\hat p\rVert\). Theorem A.2 proves this in four senses at once — the same convergence, witnessed four ways.

The engine is a single assumption (Eq. 58): the variance of \(\hat p(Y)\,W_Y\) tends to zero. From it, §S.5.2 derives convergence in probability (via Chebyshev), in mean square, in mean, and — through an \(L^1\) subsequence argument leaning on Bartle — almost everywhere. The unifying witness is the \(L^1\) distance \(\lVert p_Y-\bar p\rVert_1\): drive it to zero and all four modes follow. Slide \(M\) upward and watch the survivor's histogram collapse onto \(\bar p\).

§ 5 · Theorem A.3

How noisy it stays

Sampling from the right distribution is not enough; the estimator must also be quiet. Theorem A.3 proves that the variance of \(f(Y)\,W_Y\) converges to the variance of an estimator that drew \(X\) directly from \(\bar p\) and weighted by \(f/\bar p\) — the variance of perfect importance sampling against this target.

\[ \operatorname{Var}\bigl[f(Y)\,W_Y\bigr]\;\xrightarrow{M\to\infty}\;\operatorname{Var}_{X\sim\bar p}\!\Bigl[\tfrac{f(X)}{\bar p(X)}\Bigr]. \]

The proof is a three-step law-of-total-variance argument: the conditional variance given \(Y\) is squeezed by \(C_f^2\,\operatorname{Var}[\sum w_i]\to0\), then the gap between importance sampling against \(p_Y\) and against \(\bar p\) closes by A.2. The corollary (Item 3) is the dream: when the target is shaped like the integrand, \(\hat p\propto f\), that limiting variance is zero. Resampling toward a perfect target gives a noiseless estimate in the limit. The floor below is set by how well \(\hat p\) matches \(f\) — close the gap and watch it sink.

§ 6 · Theorem 1

The finite truth

Asymptotics reassure; practice runs at finite \(M\). Theorem 1 gives the guarantee that matters on real hardware: a closed-form envelope around the gap between the achieved variance and the ideal, in terms of one measurable quantity \(V=\operatorname{Var}\bigl[\sum_i w_i\bigr]\).

\[ \Bigl|\operatorname{Var}[f(Y)W_Y]-\operatorname{Var}_{\bar p}\!\bigl[\tfrac{f}{\bar p}\bigr]\Bigr|\;\le\;C_f^2\sqrt{V}\,\bigl(\lVert\hat p\rVert+2\sqrt{V}\bigr)\qquad\text{(S.105)} \]

The envelope is built from the same pieces as the proof of A.3: the \(\sqrt V\) factor descends from Equation S.101, the \(2\sqrt V\) from the conditional-variance bound. Its message is operational — the only thing standing between a finite run and the ideal is \(V\), the variance of the summed resampling weights, and that is something an implementation can measure and shrink. Increase \(M\); \(V\) falls, and the guaranteed band closes around the floor.

The estimator is unbiased, lands on the target, and is as quiet as perfect importance sampling — with a finite-sample guarantee. But every theorem here assumed independent samples. ReSTIR's whole trick is to reuse neighbors and previous frames, which makes samples correlated. Does any of this survive? That is the foundation the title promises.
