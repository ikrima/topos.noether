Essay 4 · ReSTIR Geometry

The Shape of a Reservoir

A reservoir is usually drawn as a tiny record: one sample, one weight, one sum. But that is just the bookkeeping shadow. Geometrically, a reservoir is a compressed shape in path space.

The useful question is not merely which sample won? It is: what probability shape did this tiny reservoir fail to preserve?
support entropy anisotropy confidence age novelty adaptive K
01 · From winner to cloud

One stored sample can hide many possible shapes.

Two reservoirs may store the same selected sample and the same weight sum, yet represent very different uncertainty. One may be a tight diffuse lobe. Another may be a long glossy ridge. A third may be two incompatible modes compressed into one unlucky winner.

Rendering translation: a one-sample reservoir is an estimator. But the history of its candidates sketches a local empirical measure: where the mass came from, how spread out it was, and whether the chosen sample is trustworthy.

02 · Transport changes density

GRIS Jacobians are shape correction, not algebraic decoration.

When a reuse map stretches path space, a fixed amount of probability mass occupies more area. When it compresses, density increases. The Jacobian is the accounting term that keeps transported mass honest.

source reservoir: μ_q transport map: T_{q→p}: Ω_q → Ω_p pushed measure: (T_{q→p})# μ_q local density change: density_new ≈ density_old / |det J_T|

The important intuition is mechanical: if the cloud is stretched into a long highlight ridge, the same sample should not pretend to represent the same density it had before transport.

03 · Confidence is not weight

A heavy sample can still be stale.

Reservoir weight says how much contribution a sample carried under a target. Confidence says whether the old target still resembles the current one. Age, mismatch, and novelty should govern reuse trust separately from contribution.

Failure mode: a stale high-weight sample can become a temporal hallucination. In rendering it appears as ghosting, stuck highlights, stale shadows, or a denoiser-like invented detail.

04 · Adaptive cardinality

Some pixels deserve more than one representative.

A flat wall can often survive with a single dominant sample. A caustic boundary, glossy ridge, or fast disocclusion cannot. The reservoir cardinality should be a local resource decision, driven by entropy, curvature, novelty, and salience.

K_p = clamp( K_min + α · entropy(R_p) + β · curvature_p + γ · novelty_p + δ · salience_p, K_min, K_max )

05 · Reservoir towers

The same shape can live at many scales.

Screen-space neighbors are only one conditional slice. If the path distribution factors by object, material, light cluster, or motion group, then reuse should climb and descend a hierarchy of conditional reservoirs.

p(path) ≈ p(object) p(surface | object) p(point | surface) p(direction | point) p(light | direction)

This is the measure-theoretic version of a production instinct: borrow from the thing that is actually stable, not merely from the thing that is adjacent on the screen.

Pixel → tile → object → scene reservoirs
Choose the reuse neighborhood by invariant, not by habit.

What to store

A production reservoir does not need to store a full distribution. It needs just enough shape metadata to decide when the selected sample is lying.

struct ShapeReservoir { Sample sample; float weightSum; float targetPdf; // Shape metadata float entropy; float supportRadius; float anisotropy; float confidence; float novelty; float curvature; uint age; // Conditional reuse identity uint objectId; uint materialId; uint lightClusterId; };

What to do with it

Use shape metadata to steer reuse trust and sample budget before the estimator has visibly failed.

float shapeRisk = a * R.entropy + b * R.anisotropy + c * R.curvature + d * R.novelty + e * agePenalty(R.age); R.reuseTrust = exp(-lambda * shapeRisk); K = chooseAdaptiveCardinality(shapeRisk, budget); if (R.reuseTrust < threshold) forceFreshProposal(p, K);
Compressed formulation: ReSTIR usually exposes the winning sample. A shape-aware ReSTIR also estimates what the reservoir had to forget: support, entropy, anisotropy, age, novelty, and curvature. These forgotten quantities are precisely the ones that tell us when reuse becomes unsafe.
