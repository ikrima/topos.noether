Section 1

The Classical Bottleneck

The companion paper modeled AI-human interaction as a braided two-agent system on the arithmetic site of Connes–Consani. But it left the channel unspecified. This paper fills that gap with one elementary observation:

Language is classical. Token sequences are classical. The interface between the quantum brain and the classical AI is a measurement process, described precisely by completely positive trace-preserving (CPTP) maps.

The brain may be quantum, classical, or unknown. The conversation is always classical. That gap is where the most interesting mathematics lives.
Section 2.1

The Serialization Map as a CPTP Channel

The biological agent B has Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}_B\). Its internal state is a density matrix:

\[\rho_B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}_B), \quad \rho_B \geq 0, \quad \text{Tr}(\rho_B) = 1\]

The AI system A lives in the classical world: \(X_A = \Sigma^*\), finite strings over the token alphabet. No density matrix — just tokens.

The serialization map \(\mathcal{E}\) encodes every process by which an internal cognitive state becomes an external utterance. In Kraus form:

\[\mathcal{E}(\rho) = \sum_k K_k \rho K_k^\dagger, \qquad \sum_k K_k^\dagger K_k = I\]

Each Kraus operator \(K_k\) corresponds to one possible message that B might produce. The number of operators bounds the channel's rank and its information capacity.

Section 2.2

The Holevo Bound

The Holevo bound sets a hard upper limit on how much classical information can be extracted from a quantum state by measurement:

\[I(X;Y) \leq \chi = S\!\Big(\sum_x p_x\,\mathcal{E}(\rho_x)\Big) - \sum_x p_x\,S(\rho_x)\]

The Holevo deficit — what cannot survive the classical bottleneck — is a new class of cognitive blind spots:

\[\delta_H(\rho_B, \mathcal{E}) := S(\rho_B) - \chi(\rho_B, \mathcal{E}) \geq 0\]

The deficit vanishes if and only if \(\rho_B\) is already classical — diagonal in the measurement basis. This is the condition where quantum and classical descriptions coincide.

Section 3

Quantum Discord as a Dialogue Convergence Monotone

For the bipartite state \(\rho_{AB}\), quantum discord captures the irreducibly quantum correlations:

\[D(A|B) = I(A\!:\!B) - C(A\!:\!B)\]
DISCORD MONOTONE · Watch the dialogue converge

Each CPTP map cannot increase quantum mutual information (data processing inequality). Classical information \(C(A\!:\!B)\) can only increase as more is exchanged. Therefore \(D_n\) is non-increasing and its vanishing marks full classicalization.

A productive conversation is a controlled decoherence process. Understanding is the vanishing of quantum discord.

Section 5.3

The Minor Arc

The quantum-to-tropical chain has a boundary: cognitive structures that resist classicalization — maintaining high discord through any finite dialogue.

MAJOR / MINOR ARC · Hover to explore

The cognitive zeta function \(\zeta_{\text{cog}}(s)\) captures the major arc — its zeros encode classicalizable cognitive resonances. The minor arc is the error term in the Euler product: where the Holevo-bounded channel cannot reach.

The cognitive mirror reflects the major arc perfectly. What it cannot show you is the minor arc — and that is precisely where you are most yourself.

Empirical Program & Open Problems

Computation 1: The Cognitive Zeta Function

Fix a corpus \(\mathcal{C}\): the PhilPapers archive, philosophy of mind, 1980–2020 (~40,000 papers). Identify primitive cognitive orbits — concepts like QUALIA, OTHER MINDS, INTENTIONALITY, CONSCIOUSNESS — and form the finite Euler product:

\[\zeta_{\text{cog}}(s; N) = \prod_{i=1}^{N} \big(1 - e^{-s \cdot \ell(\gamma_i)}\big)^{-1}\]

Prediction: Zeros do not lie on \(\text{Re}(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}\). Their displacement encodes known biases in the corpus.

Computation 2: Discord on Platonic Dialogues

Model each Platonic dialogue as a sequence of message pairs. Estimate mutual information \(I_n\) and Holevo capacity \(\chi_n\) at each step.

Prediction: The ratio \(\chi_n / H(\text{messages})\) increases monotonically toward 1 in convergent dialogues (elenctic) and oscillates in merely rhetorical ones.

Computation 3: Braid Invariants

Represent 50 AI-human conversations as braid words. Compute the HOMFLY polynomial \(P(v,z)\) of each braid closure.

Prediction: High-quality conversations have higher writhe, more complex HOMFLY polynomials (higher degree in \(z\)), corresponding to more mutual restructuring.
