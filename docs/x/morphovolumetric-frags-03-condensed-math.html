Morphovolumetric Fragmentation Site

What survives controlled cutting — a Grothendieck-style site for surface-volume exchange, with fluid dynamics as intuition scaffold

Classical topology asks how local open sets glue into a global space. This framework inverts that question: which global structures survive fragmentation into boundary/interior probes? The organizing invariant is the relationship between surface area and enclosed volume — and the natural home for this structure is not a sheaf topos but a bisheaf system over a fragmentation site.

We develop the theory through the lens of computational fluid dynamics — specifically the simulation architecture of tools like SideFX Houdini — where every core operation (voxelization, FLIP particle advection, pressure projection, surface tension) turns out to instantiate exactly the categorical structures proposed here.

§0The Aquatic Inversion

What kind of mathematics would arise if sentient aquatic mathematicians treated deformation, boundary, flow, and medium as primary, rather than point-particles and rigid objects?

The thought experiment is disarmingly simple. An intelligent civilization evolving underwater would have no reason to treat rigid particles in empty space as the obvious primitive ontology. Their mathematics might instead treat reality as made of deformations, currents, wakes, pressure gradients, boundary layers, vortices, membranes, enclosed volumes, and fluxes through surfaces.

In such an ontology, a "particle" is not primitive — it is a persistent deformation defect, a vortex that refuses to dissipate, a gluing obstruction in the descent machine. The Aquatic Topos is the formal consequence of taking this inversion seriously.

§1Surface-Volume Exchange

The organizing invariant is the relationship between a region's boundary area and its enclosed volume. In three dimensions, the scale-invariant ratio is:

\[\mathcal{I}_{SV}(U) = \frac{A(\partial U)^3}{V(U)^2}\]

But the deeper primitive is local and variational. When a boundary moves normally with displacement field \(f\):

\[\delta V = \int_{\partial U} f \, dA, \qquad \delta A = -\int_{\partial U} H f \, dA\]

where \(H\) is mean curvature. This makes curvature the local exchange rate between boundary and interior — the price at which boundary area converts to enclosed volume.

§2Morphovolumes

A morphovolume is a region equipped with surface/interior exchange structure — the tuple \(U = (|U|, \partial U, \Omega_U, \gamma_U, \chi_U)\) where \(\Omega\) is a volume form, \(\gamma\) is a boundary area measure, and \(\chi\) is the exchange law coupling them.

An enriched morphovolume may carry additional flow data: circulation \(\alpha\), vorticity \(\omega = d\alpha\), helicity \(\eta = \alpha \wedge d\alpha\), a connection \(\nabla\), flow field \(u\), shear \(\sigma\), and torsion \(\tau\).

Interactive · Morphovolume anatomy — hover layers to inspect

In Houdini terms, a morphovolume is a fluid body — a connected region of FLIP particles bounded by a level-set surface. The volume form is the particle count (or density field on the grid). The boundary is the zero-level-set of the signed distance field. The exchange law is surface tension. And the optional flow structures — vorticity, circulation, helicity — are exactly what VDB fields and particle attributes carry every substep.

§3Fragment Diagrams

The core probe is not an ordinary open subset. It is a fragment diagram — a finite cell-level decomposition recording not only which pieces exist, but how artificial internal boundaries must cancel during reconstruction.

\[D = (C, F, I, V, A, \chi)\]

where \(C\) is cells, \(F\) is boundary faces, \(I \subseteq F \times F\) is the internal face-pairing, \(V\) assigns volumes, \(A\) assigns areas, and \(\chi\) assigns exchange data.

§4Fragmentation Algebra

The central algebraic facts are simple and powerful. When a region \(U\) is fragmented into pieces \(U_i\):

\[V(U) = \sum_i V(U_i)\]

Volume is strictly additive — a cosheaf condition. But boundary area requires a correction for the internal interfaces that were created by the cut and appear twice:

\[A(\partial U) = \sum_i A(\partial U_i) - 2\sum_{e \in \mathrm{Internal}(D)} A(e)\]

This identity is the seed of the entire framework. The global isoperimetric ratio is not the sum of local ratios — you must first reconstruct extensive quantities via interface cancellation, then compute the invariant.

In Houdini, this is the voxelization step. When you convert a polygon mesh or particle cloud to a VDB grid, you fragment the continuous fluid domain into axis-aligned cells. Each voxel face shared between two fluid voxels is an internal interface. The narrow-band level-set surface is exactly the external boundary. And volume conservation — the divergence-free constraint enforced by the pressure solve — is the cosheaf condition \(V(U) = \sum_i V(U_i)\) applied every substep.

§5The Site & Bisheaves

The morphovolumetric fragmentation category \(\mathsf{MVFrag}_{SV}\) has finite fragment diagrams as objects and refinement-compatible structure-preserving maps as morphisms. The coverage \(J_{SV}\) declares a family \(\{P_i \to U\}\) a cover when the fragments jointly reconstruct volume, external boundary, area (with interface cancellation), and exchange-law compatibility — with zero residual in the exact site.

The crucial insight is that a plain sheaf is insufficient. The morphovolumetric structure contains two kinds of data that transform in opposite variance:

Sheaf Side · Intensive Data

Curvature, exchange laws, differential forms, connections — these restrict to fragments and must agree on interfaces.

\[\mathcal{F}_{SV} : \mathsf{MVFrag}_{SV}^{op} \to \mathbf{Set}\]

Contravariant. Equalizer condition: a global exchange law = compatible family of local exchange laws.

Cosheaf Side · Extensive Data

Volume, area, total flux, interface measure — these assemble covariantly with interface correction.

\[\mathcal{G}_{SV} : \mathsf{MVFrag}_{SV} \to \mathbf{Ab}_{\geq 0}\]

Covariant. Assembly: \(V(U) = \sum V(P_i)\), area with internal face subtraction.

The natural object is a morphovolumetric bisheaf:

\[(\mathcal{F}_{SV}, \mathcal{G}_{SV}, \rho)\]

where \(\rho\) is a compatibility law ensuring that assembled extensive quantities obey the local exchange laws. This is the real descent machine.

Interactive · Bisheaf duality — toggle between sheaf and cosheaf views

In Houdini's FLIP solver, the sheaf side is the velocity field and pressure gradient — local intensive data that must agree across voxel boundaries (the pressure Poisson solve enforces exactly this interface compatibility). The cosheaf side is the mass/volume carried by particles — extensive data that assembles by summation. The bisheaf compatibility \(\rho\) is the pressure projection itself: it couples the assembled density field with the local velocity to enforce divergence-free flow (the exchange law).

§6The Residual Tower

The exact site demands zero residual. But real computation — and real physics — deals in controlled approximation. The residual site \((\mathsf{MVFrag}_{SV}^\epsilon, J_{SV}^\epsilon)\) accepts covers with \(\mathrm{Res}_{SV} < \epsilon\), yielding a tower:

\[\cdots \to \mathcal{E}_{\epsilon_2} \to \mathcal{E}_{\epsilon_1} \to \mathcal{E}_0\]

for \(\epsilon_2 < \epsilon_1\). A structure that glues only at large \(\epsilon\) carries large gluing debt. A structure that glues as \(\epsilon \to 0\) is stable under controlled fragmentation.

§8Particles as Gluing Obstructions

In the point-particle ontology, particles are primitive. In the morphovolumetric ontology, particle-like objects emerge as persistent obstructions — locations where the fragmentation site's descent conditions fail in a stable, self-reinforcing way:

\[\text{particle-like event} = \text{persistent gluing obstruction under fragmentation}\]

A particle-like event appears when local fragment laws cannot glue, internal interfaces fail to cancel, curvature/exchange laws jump across an interface, flux leaks through an artificial boundary, or torsion frames fail a cocycle condition.

In Houdini, this is vortex confinement. A vortex is a topological feature of the velocity field — it has nonzero circulation around any loop enclosing it, which means any fragmentation cutting through the vortex core will have flux-leak across internal interfaces. The vortex persists under refinement — it doesn't vanish as \(\epsilon \to 0\). It is a genuine particle-like event in the morphovolumetric sense: a stable defect in the descent machine.

Instead of explaining fields using particles, explain particle-like persistence as stable defects in deformation-field descent.

The Fragment/Glue Adjunction Hypothesis

The central categorical hypothesis is that fragmentation and gluing form an adjoint pair \(\mathrm{Glue} \dashv \mathrm{Frag}\). The unit/counit measure reconstruction failure — the residuals. If the unit \(U \to \mathrm{Glue}(\mathrm{Frag}(U))\) is not an equivalence, the residual \(\mathrm{Res}_{SV}(U)\) captures unpaired interfaces, curvature jumps, flux leaks, and torsion-frame incompatibilities.

This is marked as a hypothesis, not a theorem. The research program proceeds: define the 2D perimeter/area MVP first, prove the fragmentation identities, formalize the bisheaf, specify the adjunction, then promote to 3D and stacks.

\[\boxed{\textbf{Morphovolumetric topology is the mathematics of what survives cutting.}}\]
