§3

Surface-Volume Exchange

The framework is organized around one invariant family: surface-area/volume relationships. In three dimensions, the global scale-invariant ratio is [1]:

ISV(U) = A(∂U)³ / V(U)²

But the deeper primitive is local and variational. If a boundary moves normally with displacement field f, then:

δV = ∫∂U f dA δA = −∫∂U Hf dA

Mean curvature H acts as a local exchange rate: H ~ δA/δV. The boundary is not a passive limit of a set — it is an active interface where the medium negotiates with its interior [1].

§5–6

Fragmentation Algebra

The core probe is not an ordinary open subset. It is a fragment diagram — a finite cell-level decomposition that records not only which pieces exist, but how artificial internal boundaries cancel during reconstruction [1].

Suppose a region U is fragmented into pieces Ui. Two central algebraic facts emerge:

Volume is additive

V(U) = Σᵢ V(Uᵢ)

Sum of parts equals the whole. Simple.

Boundary area needs correction

A(∂U) = Σᵢ A(∂Uᵢ) − 2·Σₑ A(e)

Internal interfaces counted twice must be subtracted. This identity is the seed of the whole framework.

To map a glued reconstruction into U is equivalent to mapping its fragment diagram into the canonical fragmentation structure of U.

§13–14

Points & Particles Reimagined

In the Aquatic Topos, a point is not a zero-dimensional primitive. It is a limiting object of fragmentations — a functorial germ [1]:

point = limit of controlled morphovolumetric fragment probes

And a particle-like event appears when local fragment laws cannot glue, internal interfaces fail to cancel, or curvature jumps persist under refinement:

particle-like event = persistent gluing obstruction under fragmentation
